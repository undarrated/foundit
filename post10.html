Best Freelancing Platforms for Remote Workers

Mika Matekenin

Feb 14, 2025 * 4 min read

Freelancing is more popular than ever, with millions of professionals opting for remote work to enjoy flexibility, independence, and diverse income streams. Whether you’re a writer, designer, developer, marketer, or consultant, freelancing platforms can connect you with global clients looking for your skills.

But with so many freelancing websites out there, which ones are the best for remote workers? In this guide, we’ll explore the top freelancing platforms to help you land consistent gigs and build a successful remote career.

1. Upwork – Best for Experienced Freelancers

Overview: Upwork is one of the largest freelancing marketplaces, connecting businesses with skilled professionals in various fields.

Key Features

  Wide range of categories (writing, design, programming, marketing, etc.)

  Secure payments through escrow system

  Opportunity for long-term contracts

  Competitive bidding system

Best For: Experienced freelancers who can showcase a strong portfolio and stand out in competitive proposals.

Pro Tip: Write detailed proposals tailored to each job and build a strong profile to attract high-paying clients.

2. Fiverr – Best for Gig-Based Freelancing

Overview: Fiverr allows freelancers to offer services (gigs) starting at $5 and scale up based on expertise and demand.

Key Features

  Fixed-price gig system (no bidding required)

  Option to set different service packages

  Great for creative professionals (graphic design, writing, video editing, etc.)

  Built-in marketing tools to promote your services

Best For: Beginners and experienced freelancers who prefer selling predefined services instead of bidding on jobs.

Pro Tip: Use SEO-friendly gig titles and descriptions to make your services more discoverable.

3. Toptal – Best for High-Level Professionals

Overview: Toptal is an exclusive network for top freelancers, particularly in tech, design, and finance. Only the top 3% of applicants are accepted.

Key Features

  High-paying clients and projects

  Strict screening process ensures quality work opportunities

  No bidding—clients come to you

  Long-term and full-time freelance opportunities available

Best For: Senior developers, designers, and finance experts looking for premium freelance work.

Pro Tip: If you have strong expertise, apply to Toptal for access to top-tier clients.

4. Freelancer – Best for Competitive Bidding

Overview: Freelancer.com offers a wide range of freelance jobs and operates on a bidding system similar to Upwork.

Key Features

  Over 1,800 job categories available

  Contests feature allows freelancers to showcase their work and win projects

  Option to communicate directly with clients before accepting a job

  Free and paid membership plans available

Best For: Freelancers who don’t mind bidding for projects and competing with a large talent pool.

Pro Tip: Stick to your niche and focus on building a strong reputation to stand out.

5. PeoplePerHour – Best for European Freelancers

Overview: PeoplePerHour is a UK-based freelancing platform connecting professionals with clients, mainly in Europe.

Key Features

  AI-powered job matching system

  Hourly and fixed-price project options

  Strong client base in Europe

  Free to join, but platform charges commission on earnings

Best For: Freelancers based in or targeting European clients.

Pro Tip: Optimize your profile with a professional headshot and portfolio to increase visibility.

6. We Work Remotely – Best for Remote Full-Time and Contract Work

Overview: Unlike traditional freelancing platforms, We Work Remotely lists high-quality remote jobs from top companies.

Key Features

  Job postings from reputable companies (e.g., Stripe, GitHub, Zapier)

  No bidding—direct job applications

  Remote-first job listings across various fields

  Strong focus on tech, marketing, and customer support roles

Best For: Freelancers looking for stable, long-term remote work instead of short-term gigs.

Pro Tip: Check the site daily, as competitive job postings fill quickly.

7. Guru – Best for Low Commission Fees

Overview: Guru is a freelancer-friendly platform with lower fees compared to competitors like Upwork.

Key Features

  Commission rates as low as 5%

  Secure and flexible payment options

  Detailed work agreements for clarity between clients and freelancers

  Ideal for IT, writing, design, and business consulting gigs

Best For: Freelancers who want to keep more of their earnings and prefer flexible payment methods.

Pro Tip: Build long-term client relationships to maximize earnings on Guru.

8. FlexJobs – Best for Remote and Flexible Job Opportunities

Overview: FlexJobs curates high-quality, remote-friendly job listings across multiple industries.

Key Features

  100% vetted job postings (no scams)

  Remote and flexible job listings

  Membership-based platform with premium features

  High-quality employers and corporate clients

Best For: Freelancers who prefer applying for legitimate, long-term remote jobs instead of short-term gigs.

Pro Tip: Invest in a premium membership for access to exclusive job listings and career coaching.

9. 99designs – Best for Designers

Overview: 99designs specializes in graphic design freelancing, allowing designers to compete for projects.

Key Features

  Clients launch design contests that freelancers can enter

  One-on-one projects for direct client work

  Ideal for logo, branding, and web design projects

  Good earning potential for skilled designers

Best For: Graphic designers, illustrators, and creative professionals.

Pro Tip: Start with contests to build credibility and attract direct client work.
